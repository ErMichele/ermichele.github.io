Upon the rim of night’s resplendent dome
A wanderer treads, by starlit whispers led.
No hearth he claims, no mortal place called home,
For all his dreams in astral fields are spread.

Yon moon, a lantern hung by Heaven’s grace,
Doth cast its argent blessing on his brow.
Through tempests vast he keeps his measured pace,
And bows to neither fate nor mortal vow.

Each sorrow borne becomes a guiding star,
Each triumph forged a beacon in the gloom.
He charts his course where distant wonders are,
And walks unshaken toward the world’s perfume.

O pilgrim bold, whose heart knows no relent,
Thy path is carved in silver firmament.
