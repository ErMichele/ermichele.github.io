The monitors hummed like distant spheres in tune,
Soft pulses echoing the rhythm of the skies.
A sterile lamp became a second moon
Suspended cold above your closing eyes.

Outside, the night was vast and unafraid,
The stars kept vigil with their ancient fire.
Inside, the world grew small, the edges frayed,
Yet something holy lingered in the wire.

Your breath, once steady as the ocean’s sweep,
Fell quiet, like a comet’s dying gleam.
I held your hand — the universe grew deep,
And time bent inward, folding like a dream.

In that still hour, grief and wonder bloomed:
Where heaven touched the hospital room.
