Beneath yon argent moon's celestial sphere, 
I tread, a soul 'midst trials vast and deep. 
Though tempest winds have whispered doubt and fear, 
The path is mine, through shadows dark and steep.

Each toil hath carved its mark upon my heart, 
A map of woes and triumphs boldly penned. 
In every loss, I claimed a nobler part, 
And found the might to rise and thus transcend.

Lo! Now I stand where stars their light bestow, 
Surveying life, its bounteous treasures wide. 
My spirit casts off shame and tears of woe, 
To join the dance where faith and hope abide.

For every climb, each rise, and humbled sprawl, 
I dare avow: I deserve it all.
