I came again to where we used to meet,
The bench still worn beneath my feet.
I spoke your name into the air,
Did I tell you that I miss you?

I brought your scarf — the one you wore
That winter when you swore
You’d fight the fever, stay with me.
I held it close. It smelled like tea.

I talked about the books you left,
The half-marked pages, dog-eared theft.
I read aloud, you didn’t speak.
I stayed until my voice grew weak.

I touched the stone, traced every line,
The dates, the name, still etched so fine.
I kissed the ground, the flowers too. 
I never said farewell to you.

I’ll never love another girl again.  
My heart still waits where yours has been.  
Each night I dream you’re back with me,  
But wake to find an empty sea.

I came again to where we used to meet,
The bench still worn beneath my feet.
I spoke your name into the air,
Did I tell you that I miss you?
